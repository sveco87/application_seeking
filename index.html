Question One · Product Problem

Vinted has a fit problem.

People post photos of clothes folded on a bed with no sense of how the item actually looks on a person. I have bought things that looked fine in the listing and just did not fit me well when they arrived. This is also an issue when it comes to selling more expensive clothes. You do not care if a $5 t-shirt fits or not, but you do care whether a $500 jacket will fit you.

I would fix this with a fit layer built into the buying experience.

Fix 01 — Try-On

Pull in Google's virtual try-on so buyers can see the item on themselves before purchasing. Not groundbreaking, but completely underused on secondhand platforms.

Fix 02 — Fit Profile

Build a fit profile from purchase history. Vinted should ask what products you own that fit well and use that to predict how new listings will fit.

Rather than filtering listings, Vinted should simply show a brief note: "These pants have a similar fit to your Tommy Hilfiger pants, however these might be slightly looser at the waist — they are a 33 and your Tommy Hilfiger are a 32."

Of course it might not work for every item and it could be a costly feature, but worth testing whether people find it useful. If they do, we could figure out the costs or even introduce it as a premium subscription feature.

Question Two · What I've Built

A furniture business. Bootstrapping to $80K after high school.

During COVID I started a furniture business. I designed small aluminium home products including wall hooks, shelves, and bike hangers, manufactured them, and sold them on Etsy. I had around 8 designs live, three of which became bestsellers in their category. I can now say I had the most sold wall hook, small bookshelf, and wall bike hanger on Etsy for a couple of months. I bootstrapped it to $80K in revenue.

But I did not have enough focus. I wanted to do everything and I was not solving the core issues that came my way.

When COVID caused factory shutdowns in China I was unable to manufacture products I had already sold. I had to refund a lot of people and got negative reviews as a result.

I originally manufactured in my home country Slovakia but moved production to China to cut costs. In hindsight that was the wrong call. I should have kept it in Slovakia, built on the premium angle, and leaned into the EU-made story. I learned more from closing that business than from anything that went well.
